Spero Dedes is one of the few sports broadcasters who stand out for their passion and energy when performing commentary during a game. The Greek-born media personality has proven that he is excellent at what he does and that is evident in his career progression. Spero who started by advertising on radio stations eventually got the chance to work with notable associations like the NFL and the NBA.

Thanks to his professional success, the sportscaster has also brought a lot of attention to his family life. Many are eager to get to know his wife, children and more. Stick with us as we reveal everything you need to know about Spero Dedes in the following 6 facts.

Bio of Spero Dedes

Spero birth details show that he was born on February 27, 1979 in Paramus, New Jersey, United States of America. He is an American citizen but his ethnicity is Greek. However, he was frugal about details of his family and formative years. There is no information about his parents or siblings.

Interestingly, Spero Dede has always had a fascination with sports. In his fledgling years, sources say he would spend a better part of the night watching the NBA league. It is possible that he also enjoyed the work of sports commentators and this could have influenced his career preference. His first job was with radio station WFAN in New York City in 2001. His stay there was short-lived, however, as Spero was employed during the same period as a radio announcer for New Jersy Gladiators.

In 2002, he worked as a backup announcer for New Jersey Nets games which aired on YES Network. He was also nominated by NBA TV and the following year he was Studio Anchor, along with several shows like The Insiders and Hardwood Classics. With the progress he had made in his earlier jobs, he was named a commentator for college basketball leagues broadcast on the CBS network.

Spero Dedes finally got a big break in his career when the NBA team, LA Lakers offered him an announcer job in 2005. Later, he was seen commentating for CBS for Super Bowl XLI. He became a staffer at CBS Sports in 2009 and was a commentator for the 2010 NCCA Tournament. He was also a commentator for the NFL, as well as the NBA during the 2010 season which aired on CBS. The sportscaster also worked for CBS 2 New York when he announced for the New York Jets in the absence of commentator, Ian Eagle.
