What is Peso Peso’s net worth? What is Peso Peso’s real name?

Introduction

Peso Peso (birth name – Mario Herrera) is an American rapper who has released five albums, having gained his stardom with the release of his first mixtape, Hardest Ese Eva, in 2019.

Herrera is most well-known for his hit singles “Fasho,” “Gummo Freestyle,” “I was trapping,” “Brad Pitt,” “Da Bottom,” and “Uber.”

His singles have a total of over 8 million streams on Spotify.

In total, the views for his songs uploaded on his YouTube channel are currently over 41 million.

Before he achieved his fame status, he released his first single, “Gotta Have It,” in 2014 under the record label Dope House Records.

He uploaded this song on YouTube. At this time, his stage name was King Pe$o.

The Origin Story


He was born in Galveston, Texas, on June 28th, 1995.

Mario has Mexican American heritage. 

Herrera had an uncertain home life at times. When he was young, his parents separated, and he moved in with his grandparents, who raised him and his three siblings from then on.

His father, Mario Herrera Senior, was mostly absent throughout Peso’s upbringing.

Peso’s father was previously also a rapper who went by the stage name Star Baby.

He was signed with Dope House Records in the 1990s and early 2000s.

Mario’s father had seen his fair share of struggle and crime throughout his life.

This may have had quite an influence on Peso, who went to jail for the first time at age 10, dropped out of high school, and started selling drugs in 9th grade.

All of this was occurring while he was beginning to make music.

Since then, Peso has been arrested several times.

Peso has claimed that he had a very violent upbringing that involved drugs and gangs when speaking on the hit podcast “No Jumper” in Los Angeles.

He has had repeat guest appearances on No Jumper, which has no doubt contributed to his fame.

First Single


Herrera released his first single in 2014 through Dope House Records, under the stage name King Pe$0.

He continued to release new songs on his YouTube, which eventually built traction and paid off.

Peso released the mixtape Bag Talk in 2017. This featured songs “Gummo Freestyle,” “Go,” “Shit Show,” “Atlanta Freestyle,” and “FWYGT.”

In 2018, he was featured in the song “Dripp Harderr” by Sauce Walka. It was uploaded by WorldStarHipHop on YouTube and has reached nearly 7 million views in total.

Peso signed on to the record label The Sauce Familia in 2019. In that same year, he released his first studio mixtape, Da Hardest Eva.

He then went on to release multiple mixtapes in 2020. Within the year, he released Salsa and Mi Vida Loka.

In 2021, Sauce Walka released the mixtapes El Patron, House Arrest, and Trappin and Killin.

He has featured on numerous tracks from other artists, most notably being “On Gang” by Soulja Boy and “Turquoise Tornado” by Riff Raff and Yelawolf.
