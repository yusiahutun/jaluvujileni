What to Look for When Buying a Shower Filter

Depending on your needs and budget, there are a few things to consider when shopping for a shower filter: cost, color and certification. Many functional in-line shower filters can be found on Amazon for less than $40. Most of those come in a silver finish, so if you have gold, brass or black finishes in your bathroom, you might want to go with an option like the AquaHome or the Jolie, which offer a variety of colors.

If you’re serious about removing as much chlorine as possible from your water supply, you’ll want to look for a water filter that’s certified to NSF/ANSI Standard 177, which guarantees that the shower filter is going to remove chlorine. Shower filters, like the Jolie model, is certified to that standard. Those options, however, are going to have a higher price point.

FAQ

Do shower filters work?

In short, yes, shower filters do work. One of the main reasons for using a shower filter is to remove chlorine, which can negatively affect skin, hair, throats and lungs. While some experts say that shower filters don’t treat all the water quality issues they claim to, most do well at managing chlorine-related issues, which can specifically affect the skin and hair.

Is a shower filter good for hair?

Shower filters are vital for removing excess chlorine from your water, which affects hair. Your hair (and skin) will be dry and damaged if too much chlorine absorbs into the follicles.

How long do shower filters last?

Shower filters typically last for six months, depending on your chosen filter. Although some filters can last longer, we recommend regular replacement to ensure effectiveness.
