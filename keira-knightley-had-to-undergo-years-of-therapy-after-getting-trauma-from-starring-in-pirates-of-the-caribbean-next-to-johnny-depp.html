Keira Knightley Had to Undergo 'Years of Therapy' after Getting 'Trauma' from Starring in Pirates of

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, February 7, 2024

Keira Knightley, renowned for her performances in films like The Imitation Game, Colette, and Pirates of the Caribbean, recently opened up about the profound impact her role in the Pirates of the Caribbean franchise had on her mental well-being. In a resurfaced interview, Knightley discussed the “many years of therapy” she underwent following her involvement in the successful film series. While Knightley’s career has spanned various remarkable roles, the scrutiny and fame that came with Pirates of the Caribbean had a lasting effect on her.

The Rise to Fame and Uncomfortable Age Gaps

Keira Knightley first gained recognition through her breakout role in Bend It Like Beckham and further endeared herself to audiences with her performance in the Christmas classic, Love, Actually. However, as fans delved into the details, they couldn’t help but notice a troubling age gap in some of her films.

In Love, Actually, Knightley played a married woman caught in a heart-wrenching love triangle alongside Thomas Brodie Sangster, who portrayed Liam Neeson’s movie son. Despite the characters’ significant age difference, the actors were only five years apart, which raised eyebrows among viewers.

A Teenager in the Spotlight

As the focus shifted to Knightley’s involvement in Pirates of the Caribbean, the internet became unsettled by the revelation that she was just seventeen years old during the filming of The Curse of the Black Pearl. The discomfort intensified as Knightley’s character shared a romantic connection with Orlando Bloom’s character, despite the notable age disparity.

Reflecting on her experience, Knightley admitted that the intense scrutiny and fame she faced during that period were deeply challenging.

In a 2016 interview with Variety, she confessed, “I found it pretty horrific. I’m not an extrovert, so I found that level of scrutiny and that level of fame really hard… It was traumatic, but it set up the rest of my career.”

Years of Therapy and Personal Growth

The psychological toll of her early success in Pirates of the Caribbean was significant for Knightley. She revealed that she sought therapy for many years to cope with the aftermath of the role and the accompanying fame. The experience left a lasting impact on her, both personally and professionally.

“I’m not an extrovert, so I found that level of scrutiny and that level of fame really hard… It was traumatic, but it set up the rest of my career.”

– Keira Knightley
