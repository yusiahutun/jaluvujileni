Lone Star' Season 5 Begins Production

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, January 21, 2024

The Big Picture

  • The cast of 9-1-1: Lone Star is pumped for Season 5 as they begin filming, sharing their excitement on social media.
  • Rafael Silva, Natacha Karam, and Ronen Rubinstein kick off filming with sentimental messages and behind-the-scenes glimpses.
  • Although details of the first day's scenes are under wraps, set pictures show that the cast is thrilled to be back together.

The cast of 9-1-1: Lone Star is back together as production begins for the fifth season of the delayed firefighter drama series. The cast took to their social media accounts to express excitement at being back, with some sharing sentimental messages about what filming the fifth season means to them. According to posts by Rafael Silva, Natacha Karam, and Ronen Rubinstein, today was the first day of filming as they recorded the first scenes for the season. While they didn't share what exactly they filmed, the set pictures and videos shared showed them and other actors happy on their first day.

Silva expressed gratitude in his Instagram Story at being able to film the delayed season. He reiterated that it had been a while since they had filmed anything and was appreciative of the fact that 5 seasons was not a minor feat for shows nowadays. 9-1-1: Lone Star's fate has been uncertain since the shake-up in Fox's schedule that saw the network cancel their top-rated drama, 9-1-1. At that time, the double strikes were raging on, something that saw several TV shows fall victim and get canceled. The Season 4 finale aired mid-May last year, leaving the show's fate in the air for over six months. Even when Fox renewed the show for a fifth season, there were some caveats, including a shorter episode order, and a fall 2024 premiere.

Karam was excited to get back to work, commenting that she would "be American" for the next few months referencing the distinction between her American character and her own Irish-Lebanese nationality. She shared a picture with cast mates Brianna Baker and Brian Michael Smith in front of the fire engine. Rubinstein shared a selfie of himself smiling while wearing his paramedic uniform with the caption "T.K 5.0." Baker also shared a photo of Karam and Julian Works on set, confirming that all the junior first responders were back.

About '9-1-1: Lone Star' Season 5

Close

9-1-1: Lone Star will stay at Fox even as the 9-1-1 moves to ABC. The only change in the season is that it will be 12 episodes long -- 6 episodes less than Season 4, and what the production company wanted to sell to Fox. The season is expected to premiere this fall during the 2024/2025 schedule. Details about major storylines in the season are not out yet but Episode 1 is called "Both Sides, Now" according to script images shared by the cast on social media.

The show stars Rob Lowe, Gina Torres, Rubinstein, Sierra McClain, Jim Parrack, Karam, Michael Smith, Silva, Works, Baker, Kelsey Yates and Skyler Yates.

9-1-1: Lone Star

A spin-off series of 9-1-1, 9-1-1: Lone Star is an action-drama series created for Fox. The series follows Rob Lowe as Owen Strand, a firefighter from New York City who, after having rebuilt a team in the aftermath of September 11th's attacks, is brought in to form a new one in Austin, Texas.
