Is 'Attack on Titan Requiem' Part of the Official Story? Alternate Ending and Canon Connection Expla

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, January 27, 2024

When the anime series “Attack on Titan” concluded, a new conversation emerged within its fan community, focusing on a term that intrigued and confused many: “Attack on Titan Requiem”. This fan-made series presents an alternate ending to the beloved story, diverging notably from Hajime Isayama’s original vision. But the burning question among fans remains: Is “Attack on Titan Requiem” canon?

The Verdict on Canon Status

To set the record straight, “Attack on Titan Requiem” is not part of the canon storyline crafted by Hajime Isayama. This project emerges from the depths of fan creativity, offering a distinct closure to the series that doesn’t end with the protagonist Eren’s demise. It’s a testament to the series’ impact on its audience, showcasing their dedication and imaginative prowess.

The Essence of Attack on Titan Requiem

“Attack on Titan Requiem” is more than just a fan-made tribute; it’s a reimagining of the series’ climax. In contrast to the original ending, where about 80% of the world’s population is decimated and Eren meets his end at Mikasa Ackerman’s hands, this alternate universe takes a different path. The fan creation is partially inspired by a music video titled “Akatsuki no Requiem”, released three years ago, hinting at an alternate conclusion to Eren’s journey.

The Direction of the Alternate Ending

In this reimagined world, Eren’s fate diverges significantly. He emerges victorious against the Allies, continues with the Rumbling, and obliterates the population outside Paradis Island. This stark contrast paints a picture of triumph and tragedy, marked by the demise of key characters who stood in Eren’s way.

The Craftsmanship Behind the Project

Despite its unofficial status, “Attack on Titan Requiem” is remarkable in its quality. It mirrors the original manga’s art style with impressive accuracy, making it a must-see for fans. With three parts already released, and parts 4 and 5 in development, pending funding, the project promises to delve deeper into this alternate reality.

Where to Experience the Requiem Series

For enthusiasts eager to explore this fan-made universe, the first three parts of “Attack on Titan Requiem” are available on its official website. Updates about the upcoming parts are shared on X (formerly Twitter), keeping fans in the loop.

The Legacy of Fan-Made Content

“Attack on Titan Requiem” exemplifies the power of fan creativity in extending the life and narrative of beloved series. As 2023 unfolds, stay connected for more intriguing developments in the anime and manga world.
