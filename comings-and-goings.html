Who Plays Georgie on General Hospital?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, July 11, 2024

If Maxie and Spinelli’s daughter Georgie looked a little different, a little more grown up on the July 27 episode of General Hospital, that’s because she’s now being played by 14-year-old Eden Harker. Harker takes over from Lily Fisher, who last aired the first week of April during The Nurses Ball. The role has also been played by Harper Barash in 2015, the real-life daughter of Kirsten Storms (Maxie) and Brandon Barash, former Johnny Zacchara, now Stefan DiMera on Days of our Lives.

Harker is known for the Lifetime television movie Pom Poms Payback, about a group of cheerleaders seeking revenge for being betrayed by their boyfriends. She has also voiced the character of Little Laura on Nickelodeon’s Rug Rats. Aside from acting, Harker enjoys dancing, singing, and painting.

Georgie is the daughter of Maxie Jones and Damian Spinelli and was the center of a surrogacy scandal. Maxie was supposed to be carrying Lulu and Dante Falconeri’s child. However, she lost that child after tripping and soon became pregnant with Spinelli’s baby during a one-night stand. She told no one about the miscarriage or who the father of the child she was carrying was, which was eventually revealed by Spinelli’s girlfriend Ellie Trout. A custody battle ensued over the child, with Spinelli winning full custody of the baby. Maxie was denied visitation rights to her daughter until much later, with Spinelli and Ellie living in Portland with the little girl. However, after the break-up of Ellie and Spinelli, Maxie and Spinelli have been sharing custody of their daughter in Port Charles.

Currently, Maxie’s small cramped apartment has been a source of contention between Georgie, and her younger brother James. Forced to share a room, they’ve been at one another’s throats, which has only become worse as Georgie is now entering her teen years. Maxie has been on the hunt for a larger home but has been reluctant to give up her apartment which is her last link to James’ father, the late Nathan West.
